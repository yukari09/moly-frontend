### Overall Website Concept & Tone

**Concept: "Your Passport to Global Culture"**

The website, "DayCal," will be designed as a digital magazine and an interactive atlas of human celebration. It's not just a calendar; it's a window into the soul of global communities. The design will balance vibrant, high-impact visuals with a clean, intuitive interface, making deep cultural exploration feel both exciting and effortless.

* **Visual Style:**
    * **Imagery:** Rich, professional, and authentic photography and videography will be central. The style will be immersive and evocative, similar to National Geographic or Condé Nast Traveler.
    * **Color Palette:** A base of clean whites and dark charcoals to let the content shine, accented by a vibrant, multicultural palette (e.g., marigold yellow, turquoise, ruby red) used for calls-to-action, filters, and highlights.
    * **Typography:** A modern, highly-readable serif font (like 'Lora' or 'Merriweather') for storytelling and body text to convey authority and elegance. This will be paired with a clean, geometric sans-serif font (like 'Montserrat' or 'Lato') for UI elements, headings, and CTAs to ensure clarity and usability.

* **Emotional Tone:** Inspiring, curious, respectful, and authentic. The copy will be written to spark wonder and encourage discovery. It should feel like a recommendation from a well-traveled, culturally-savvy friend rather than an automated aggregator.

---

### Key Pages & Their Purpose

1.  **Home:** The gateway to discovery. Its goal is to immediately captivate the user, communicate the unique value proposition (story-driven discovery), and guide them toward the core features.
2.  **Explore Festivals (Interactive Calendar):** The primary tool of the website. This page allows users to search, filter, and discover festivals based on their interests, dates, and location. Its goal is to provide a powerful yet simple-to-use discovery engine.
3.  **Festival Detail Page (Template):** The heart of the content. Each festival will have its own dedicated page. The goal is to provide a comprehensive, educational, and visually stunning deep-dive that informs and inspires.
4.  **Stories (Blog/Content Hub):** The editorial section. This page houses long-form articles, listicles, and cultural guides that don't fit into a single festival entry (e.g., "10 Harvest Festivals to Visit in Autumn," "The History of Lantern Festivals"). Its goal is to drive SEO, establish authority, and provide added value for returning users.
5.  **About Us:** The "Why" page. It tells the story behind DayCal, states its mission to connect people through cultural understanding, and builds trust with the audience.
6.  **Contribute:** The community-building page. Its goal is to empower users to become part of the project by submitting festivals or content, fostering a sense of ownership and helping the database grow organically.

### Content Sections Per Page

#### 1. Home Page
* **Hero Section:** A full-screen, stunning video montage or a powerful hero image of a vibrant festival.
    * **Headline:** "Discover the World's Celebrations."
    * **Sub-headline:** "Your guide to the stories, traditions, and dates behind global festivals."
    * **Primary CTA / Search Bar:** A prominent search bar with the prompt "Find a festival, country, or tradition..." and a "Explore" button.
* **Featured Festivals:** A curated, visually-driven carousel showcasing "Happening This Month," "Trending Festivals," or "Editor's Picks." Each card links to a Festival Detail Page.
* **Discover by Interest (Category Browser):** A clean grid of icons or images representing key categories (e.g., Music, Food & Drink, Religious, Cultural, Arts, Seasonal).
* **Explore by Region (Interactive Map):** A simplified world map with highlighted regions. Clicking a region filters the "Explore Festivals" page.
* **From Our Stories (Blog Preview):** A section featuring the 3 most recent or popular articles from the "Stories" hub to showcase the depth of content.
* **Newsletter Signup CTA:** A visually appealing, simple form with a compelling reason to subscribe: "Get a weekly dose of culture delivered to your inbox."

#### 2. Explore Festivals Page
* **Header Section:** Includes the main search bar and prominent filter controls.
    * **Filters:** Date Range, Region/Country, Categories, and Tags (e.g., Family-Friendly, Free Admission, Once in a Lifetime).
* **Primary View (Interactive Calendar):** A large, clean monthly calendar view. Festivals are represented by small, colored dots or images. Hovering reveals a quick summary, and clicking navigates to the detail page. Users can toggle to a Weekly or List view.
* **Secondary View (Card/List Layout):** Below or adjacent to the calendar, search results appear as visually rich cards, each with a primary image, festival name, date, location, and a short blurb.

#### 3. Festival Detail Page (Template)
* **Hero Section:** A captivating main image or short video of the festival.
    * **Title:** Name of the Festival (e.g., "Holi - The Festival of Colors").
    * **Key Info Box:** A sticky sidebar or a prominent box at the top with essential data:
        * **Date(s):** e.g., 'March 17, 2026'
        * **Location:** City, Country
        * **Type:** Cultural, Religious
        * **Tags:** Family-Friendly, Outdoors
* **Main Content (Tabbed or Long-scroll):**
    * **The Story:** The history, origins, and cultural significance. This is where the core storytelling happens.
    * **Traditions & What to Expect:** A guide to the key activities, rituals, food, and music.
    * **Traveler's Guide:** Practical tips on getting there, where to stay, what to wear, and local etiquette.
* **Gallery:** A beautiful masonry grid of professional photos and user-submitted images (future feature).
* **Social Share & Save:** Prominent buttons to share the page or "Add to My Calendar" (future feature).

#### 4. Stories (Blog) Page
* **Featured Article:** A large banner for the latest or most important article.
* **Article Grid:** A chronological grid of all other articles, displayed as cards with an image, title, and excerpt.
* **Category Filter:** A sidebar or dropdown menu to filter articles by topic (e.g., Travel Guides, Cultural Deep Dives, Interviews).

### Target Audience Appeal

This design directly addresses the pain points of the target audience:

* **For Culture Enthusiasts & Travelers (25-45):** The emphasis on high-quality visuals and "The Story" behind each festival satisfies their desire for deep, authentic cultural insights beyond a simple date and location. The "Traveler's Guide" section provides immediate, practical value for trip planning. The clean, responsive UI respects their tech-savviness.
* **For Families & Educators:** The ability to filter by "Family-Friendly" is a key functional benefit. The educational focus, especially in the "History" and "Traditions" sections, provides a reliable and engaging resource for teaching children about world cultures.
* **For Students:** The in-depth, well-structured content serves as a more engaging and credible starting point for research than generic sources. The clear separation of history, traditions, and practical info makes information easy to parse.

### Primary Call-to-Action (CTA)

**Primary CTA: "Explore Festivals"**

This is the most important action we want users to take. It encourages immediate engagement with the website's core tool and value proposition.

* **Placement:**
    1.  **Homepage Hero Section:** The main button after the search bar.
    2.  **Main Navigation Bar:** A permanent, button-styled link in the header, visible on every page.
    3.  **End of Articles:** Within "Stories" articles, a contextual CTA like "Inspired? Explore more cultural festivals now."
